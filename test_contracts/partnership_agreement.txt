1. PARTNERSHIP NAME AND BUSINESS
1.1 The partnership shall conduct business under the name "Mauritius Ventures Trading".
1.2 The principal business shall be the import and export of electronic goods and accessories.
1.3 The registered address shall be 45 John Kennedy Street, Port Louis, Mauritius.

2. CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS
2.1 The total initial capital of the partnership shall be MUR 3,000,000 (Three Million Mauritian Rupees).
2.2 The Partners shall contribute capital as follows:
    - Partner A: MUR 1,500,000 (50%)
    - Partner B: MUR 900,000 (30%)
    - Partner C: MUR 600,000 (20%)
2.3 Additional capital contributions may only be made with unanimous consent of all Partners.

3. PROFIT AND LOSS SHARING
3.1 Profits and losses shall be shared in proportion to capital contributions:
    - Partner A: 50%
    - Partner B: 30%
    - Partner C: 20%
3.2 Profit distributions shall be made quarterly, within 30 days of each quarter end.
3.3 A minimum of 30% of annual profits shall be retained for working capital and business development.

4. MANAGEMENT
4.1 Partner A shall serve as Managing Partner with responsibility for day-to-day operations.
4.2 The Managing Partner shall receive a monthly salary of MUR 80,000 in addition to profit share.
4.3 Major decisions requiring approval of Partners holding at least 70% interest include:
    (a) Capital expenditure exceeding MUR 500,000
    (b) Contracts with value exceeding MUR 1,000,000
    (c) Hiring employees with monthly salary exceeding MUR 100,000
    (d) Opening new business locations
    (e) Obtaining loans or credit facilities
4.4 The following decisions require unanimous consent:
    (a) Admission of new partners
    (b) Amendment of this Agreement
    (c) Sale or dissolution of the partnership

5. DUTIES AND RESTRICTIONS
5.1 Each Partner shall:
    (a) Devote sufficient time and attention to partnership business
    (b) Act honestly and in good faith in all partnership matters
    (c) Maintain accurate records of all transactions
    (d) Not engage in any business that competes with the partnership
5.2 No Partner shall, without unanimous consent:
    (a) Assign or transfer their partnership interest
    (b) Use partnership funds for personal purposes
    (c) Bind the partnership to any guarantee, indemnity, or surety

6. LIABILITY
6.1 Each Partner shall be jointly and severally liable for all debts and obligations of the partnership.
6.2 This means each Partner is personally responsible for the full amount of any partnership debt, regardless of their capital contribution.
6.3 Creditors may pursue any Partner for the entire debt without first pursuing other Partners.
6.4 A Partner who pays more than their share of a debt may seek contribution from the other Partners.

7. BANKING
7.1 Partnership bank accounts shall be maintained at MCB Ltd, Port Louis.
7.2 Any withdrawal or payment exceeding MUR 100,000 shall require signatures of at least two Partners.
7.3 The Managing Partner may authorize payments up to MUR 100,000 individually.

8. ACCOUNTS AND RECORDS
8.1 Proper books of account shall be maintained at the partnership's registered office.
8.2 Annual accounts shall be prepared within 90 days of each financial year end.
8.3 Accounts shall be audited by an independent certified accountant.
8.4 Each Partner shall have the right to inspect books and records upon reasonable notice.

9. WITHDRAWAL
9.1 A Partner may withdraw from the partnership by giving 180 days written notice to the other Partners.
9.2 Upon withdrawal, the withdrawing Partner shall be entitled to:
    (a) Return of their capital contribution
    (b) Their share of profits up to the date of withdrawal
    (c) A fair value of their share of partnership goodwill as determined by independent valuation
9.3 Payment to the withdrawing Partner may be made over a period of 12 months.
9.4 A withdrawing Partner shall not compete with the partnership within Mauritius for 24 months following withdrawal.

10. EXPULSION
10.1 A Partner may be expelled by unanimous vote of the other Partners for:
    (a) Material breach of this Agreement
    (b) Fraud, dishonesty, or gross misconduct
    (c) Conviction of a criminal offense
    (d) Bankruptcy or insolvency
    (e) Conduct materially prejudicial to the partnership business
10.2 An expelled Partner shall receive fair value of their interest, less any damages caused to the partnership.

11. DEATH OR INCAPACITY
11.1 Upon the death or permanent incapacity of a Partner, the partnership shall not automatically dissolve.
11.2 The surviving Partners shall have the option to purchase the deceased Partner's share at fair market value.
11.3 If the option is not exercised within 90 days, the deceased Partner's heirs may:
    (a) Become partners with unanimous consent of surviving Partners, or
    (b) Receive payment for the value of the deceased Partner's share over 12 months

12. DISSOLUTION
12.1 The partnership shall dissolve upon:
    (a) Unanimous agreement of all Partners
    (b) Order of a court of competent jurisdiction
    (c) Bankruptcy of the partnership
12.2 Upon dissolution, partnership assets shall be applied in the following order:
    (a) Payment of all partnership debts and liabilities
    (b) Repayment of loans made by Partners to the partnership
    (c) Return of capital contributions
    (d) Distribution of any surplus in accordance with profit-sharing ratios

13. INDEMNIFICATION
13.1 Each Partner shall indemnify and hold harmless the other Partners from any liability arising from their unauthorized acts or breaches of this Agreement.
13.2 This indemnification shall include legal fees and expenses.

14. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
14.1 Any dispute between Partners shall first be submitted to mediation.
14.2 If mediation fails within 21 days, the dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration.
14.3 The arbitration shall be conducted in Port Louis under the rules of the Mauritius International Arbitration Centre.
14.4 The decision of the arbitrator shall be final and binding on all parties.

15. GENERAL
15.1 This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the Partners.
15.2 No amendment shall be valid unless in writing and signed by all Partners.
15.3 This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of Mauritius.
