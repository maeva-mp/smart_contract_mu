COMMERCIAL LEASE AGREEMENT

This Lease Agreement is made on 1st April 2024

BETWEEN:
Mauritian Properties Ltd
Registered Office: 10 Edith Cavell Street, Port Louis, Mauritius
Company Number: C05034567
(hereinafter referred to as "the Landlord")

AND:
Island Café & Restaurant Ltd
Registered Office: Royal Road, Grand Baie, Mauritius
Company Number: C19056789
(hereinafter referred to as "the Tenant")

PROPERTY:
Ground Floor, Harbour View Building
15 Caudan Waterfront, Port Louis, Mauritius
Floor Area: 250 square meters

1. TERM
1.1 The lease shall commence on 1st May 2024 and continue for a period of five (5) years.
1.2 The Tenant shall have the option to renew for an additional five (5) years by giving 180 days written notice before expiry.
1.3 Renewal terms shall be negotiated in good faith, provided the rent increase shall not exceed 15% of the then-current rent.

2. RENT
2.1 Monthly Rent: MUR 120,000 (One Hundred Twenty Thousand Mauritian Rupees)
2.2 Payment Due: On or before the 5th day of each calendar month
2.3 Payment Method: Bank transfer to the Landlord's designated account
2.4 Late Payment: A penalty of 2% per week shall apply to any rent paid after the due date
2.5 Rent Review: The Landlord may review rent annually on the anniversary date, with increases not exceeding 5% per annum.

3. SECURITY DEPOSIT
3.1 The Tenant shall pay a security deposit of MUR 360,000 (equivalent to three months' rent) upon signing.
3.2 The deposit shall be held by the Landlord without interest.
3.3 The deposit shall be refunded within 30 days of lease termination, less any amounts due for repairs, unpaid rent, or other obligations.

4. PERMITTED USE
4.1 The premises shall be used solely as a café and restaurant.
4.2 The Tenant shall obtain all necessary licenses, permits, and approvals at its own expense.
4.3 The Tenant shall comply with all applicable health, safety, and fire regulations.
4.4 The Tenant shall not conduct any illegal, immoral, or hazardous activities on the premises.

5. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS
5.1 The Landlord shall be responsible for:
    (a) Structural repairs to the roof, walls, and foundations
    (b) Major repairs to plumbing and electrical systems
    (c) Maintenance of common areas
5.2 The Tenant shall be responsible for:
    (a) Day-to-day maintenance and cleanliness of the premises
    (b) Minor repairs and replacements (up to MUR 25,000 per incident)
    (c) All fixtures, fittings, and equipment installed by the Tenant
    (d) Pest control within the premises

6. ALTERATIONS
6.1 The Tenant shall not make any structural alterations without the Landlord's prior written consent.
6.2 Non-structural alterations may be made with prior notice to the Landlord.
6.3 All approved alterations shall become the property of the Landlord upon termination.
6.4 The Landlord may require the Tenant to restore the premises to its original condition at the Tenant's expense.

7. INSURANCE
7.1 The Landlord shall maintain building insurance covering fire, flood, and structural damage.
7.2 The Tenant shall maintain and provide evidence of:
    (a) Public liability insurance with minimum coverage of MUR 5,000,000
    (b) Contents insurance for all stock, equipment, and fixtures
    (c) Business interruption insurance
7.3 The Tenant shall indemnify and hold harmless the Landlord against all claims, damages, and liabilities arising from the Tenant's use of the premises.

8. ASSIGNMENT AND SUBLETTING
8.1 The Tenant shall not assign this lease or sublet the premises without the Landlord's prior written consent.
8.2 Such consent shall not be unreasonably withheld but may be subject to conditions.
8.3 Notwithstanding any assignment, the Tenant shall remain fully liable for all obligations under this lease.

9. DEFAULT
9.1 The following shall constitute events of default:
    (a) Failure to pay rent within 14 days of the due date
    (b) Breach of any term of this lease not remedied within 21 days of written notice
    (c) The Tenant becoming insolvent, bankrupt, or entering liquidation
    (d) Abandonment of the premises
    (e) Use of the premises for any unlawful purpose
9.2 Upon default, the Landlord may:
    (a) Terminate this lease immediately
    (b) Re-enter and take possession of the premises
    (c) Claim damages for breach
    (d) Forfeit the security deposit

10. EARLY TERMINATION
10.1 If the Tenant terminates this lease before the end of the term without cause, the Tenant shall pay liquidated damages equal to six (6) months' rent.
10.2 The parties agree this amount represents a genuine pre-estimate of the Landlord's losses.

11. INDEMNIFICATION
11.1 The Tenant shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Landlord from and against all claims, actions, damages, liability, and expenses arising from:
    (a) The Tenant's use and occupation of the premises
    (b) Any breach of this lease by the Tenant
    (c) Any negligent or wrongful act of the Tenant, its employees, or invitees
11.2 This indemnification shall survive termination of this lease.

12. LANDLORD'S ACCESS
12.1 The Landlord may enter the premises upon 24 hours notice for inspection, repairs, or showing to prospective tenants.
12.2 In case of emergency, the Landlord may enter without notice.

13. FORCE MAJEURE
13.1 Neither party shall be liable for failure to perform due to events beyond reasonable control.
13.2 Rent shall abate proportionally during any period the premises are unfit for use due to force majeure.

14. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
14.1 Disputes shall first be submitted to mediation.
14.2 If mediation fails within 30 days, disputes shall be resolved by arbitration.
14.3 The seat of arbitration shall be Port Louis, Mauritius.

15. GENERAL
15.1 This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties.
15.2 No amendment shall be valid unless in writing and signed by both parties.
15.3 This lease shall be governed by the laws of Mauritius.
15.4 The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the Courts of Mauritius.
